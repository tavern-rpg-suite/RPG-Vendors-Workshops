# RPG Vendors & Workshops

A SillyTavern extension that fills your world with **vendors, crafters and trainers** — a card‑file of characters who **sell goods**, hand out **quests**, **repair** and **sharpen** your gear, teach you a **skill**, and run a full **crafting bench**. Everything (stock, recipes, ingredients, quests, lessons) is generated by the model to fit your setting.

> Part of the RPG suite. It plugs into the **Inventory** (`window.RPG.inventory` / `window.RPG.quest`), **Equipment** (`window.RPG.equipment`), **Vitals** (`window.RPG.vitals`) and **Map** (`window.RPG.map`) modules when they're present, and works with whatever subset you have installed.

**Version 1.32.0**

---

## ✨ What a vendor can do

Each vendor is a character card with a **type** (cook, apothecary, tailor, blacksmith, jeweller, merchant, **trainer**, or custom) and tabs:

- 🛒 **Shop** — 4–5 goods that fit the trade, era and personality, with prices and weights. **Re‑rolling doesn't repeat itself** — the vendor remembers what it has offered and the model is told to bring fresh stock.
- 📜 **Jobs** — 4–5 short, character‑specific side‑quests with **varied rewards** (guaranteed at least one **coins** and one **buff**, plus items and **repair**). Accept → roleplay → resolve; a hidden note goes to the chat so the vendor "knows".
- 🔧 **Repair** — restore durability on broken gear (menders only: tailors, blacksmiths, merchants, custom).
- 📖 **Recipes** — learn recipes for coins. For‑sale recipes show a tempting **flavour** teaser (ingredients hidden until learned); **prices scale by star tier** (1★ ~15 → 5★ ~150+). Blacksmiths/tailors also get **⚒ sharpening** and **🛠 repair‑kit** recipes.
- ⚗️ **Craft** — a full three‑panel **workbench** (see below). For **trainers** this tab becomes **Training**.

## ⚗️ Crafting bench

A triptych: **Recipes** · a **circle** you fill with ingredients · your **Ingredients**.

- **Recipe crafting** — place the recipe's ingredients in the circle, play a quick **mini‑game**, and get the item. Food gains **satiety + an effect on eating**; gear carries a **buff applied when worn** (via the Equipment module).
- **Freestyle** — combine any 2–4 materials with no recipe. The model judges whether the mix **fits this vendor's trade** (copper in a cook's pot won't), then produces a result. **Failed or sloppy crafts** yield a wryly‑named **"botched" item** whose **debuff** triggers on eating/wearing (and clears when removed).
- **Ingredients** — needed ingredients come from the recipe. Collect them by taking a **foraging quest** and finding them with the inventory **magnifier** (works with the Map for room‑based odds, and without it by scene/skill), or just **buy** a few from the shop.

## ⚒️ Sharpening & 🛠 repair (with the Equipment module)

Blacksmiths (weapons) and tailors (armour) generate **sharpening recipes** per target grade (→Honed / →Fine / →Legendary) and **repair‑kit recipes** per tier. On the bench you pick a target — **equipped or in your backpack** — fill its ingredients, and run the mini‑game:

- **Sharpen** raises the item one **grade** and restores durability. Reaching **Legendary (3→4) can fail** and burns the materials.
- **Repair kit** restores durability on a damaged piece.

## 🎓 Trainers — a skill tree

Trainers teach a discipline (swordsmanship, an instrument, marksmanship, magic…) and level a **skill 1–5**:

- **2 main lessons per level** gate progression — finish both to rise a level (which grants a **permanent perk** + a little max‑HP and opens the next level's lessons). A **graduation** challenge waits at level 5 with a signature reward.
- **2–3 themed drills** per level (mostly roleplay, occasionally a mini‑game), **regeneratable** for extra XP.
- Lessons/drills run as real quests: **Take → play it out → "I did it"** (trust) or **Check** (the AI reviews the scene **from where you took it**). A settings toggle can require the AI check. **Declining** a main lesson gives a debuff and swaps in a new one.
- Rewards vary: **XP, a buff, or an item** (buffs/gear feed Vitals/Equipment). Hidden chat notes (with the task's description) keep the trainer aware.

## 🎮 Mini‑games

Three rotate at random so it stays fresh: **timing arc**, **reaction**, and **memory sequence**. Real misses don't count, and a botched attempt gives no reward.

## 🧰 Living world touches

- **Broken‑gear drop** — sometimes at the start of a chat a broken item falls into your pack and a **repair quest** opens at a fitting vendor (with a chat note).
- **Per‑vendor progress** — recipes, skill, training and stock are stored **separately per vendor** and preserved across edits.

## 📦 Install

Copy the `RPG-Vendors-Workshops` folder into your third‑party extensions folder (e.g. `SillyTavern/data/<user>/extensions/`), reload, and enable it in **Extensions → RPG Vendors & Workshops**.

## ⚙️ Setup

1. Tick **Enable** and pick a **Language**.
2. Fill in an OpenAI‑compatible **URL / API Key / Model** (default `google/gemma-4-31b-it`).
3. Optional toggles: **write quest/shop notes into the chat**, and **field lessons require an AI review** (hides "I did it").
4. Create a vendor from a character card (name / type / description — there's a **Generate description** helper), then use its tabs.

## 🔌 Cross‑extension bridges it uses

- `window.RPG.inventory` — items, coins, and `update()` for grades in the backpack.
- `window.RPG.quest` — starts foraging hunts the magnifier can fulfil.
- `window.RPG.equipment` — `sharpenable / getGrade / sharpen / repair` for grades & mending.
- `window.RPG.vitals` — buffs/perks, healing, permanent (`duration:null`) rewards, `setHp`.
- `window.RPG.map` — assigns ingredients to real rooms so foraging is location‑aware.

Anything missing is simply skipped — the module degrades gracefully.

## 🌍 Language & saving

Bilingual (RU / EN); all state saved per chat.

## 🩺 Troubleshooting

- **The Craft tab was blank.** Fixed in 1.27.1 — each column renders independently, so one hiccup can't blank the tab (details land in the console).
- **The extension icon/settings vanished after an update.** Fixed in 1.29.1 — a duplicate function broke module load; the build is now validated as an ES module.
- **Sharpen/repair recipes looked empty.** Fixed in 1.32.0 — they now show a clear description; their ingredients still stay hidden until learned.
- **"Reward:" toast was empty.** Fixed in 1.30.x — a duplicated i18n key was overriding the reward text.
- **Learning a recipe at one vendor changed another.** Vendors are independent; 1.29.0 also hardened editing so per‑vendor recipes/skill/training are never wiped.
- **Generation does nothing.** Shop, recipes, quests, crafting and training each need a working URL / key / model.
